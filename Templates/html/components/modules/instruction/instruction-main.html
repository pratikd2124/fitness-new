    Warm up:

    Begin your workout with a proper warm-up routine. This can include light cardiovascular exercises like jogging or cycling, as well as dynamic stretches to prepare your muscles for the workout ahead.*

    Use proper form:

    When performing exercises, focus on using correct form and technique. Poor form can lead to injuries and decrease the effectiveness of the exercise. If you're unsure about proper form, ask a qualified trainer for assistance.

  • Start with lighter weights

    If you're new to weightlifting or trying a new exercise, start with lighter weights to build a foundation of strength and proper technique. Gradually increase the weight as you become more comfortable and stronger.

    Use appropriate equipment:

    Select the appropriate equipment for your workout. Adjust machines or equipment to fit your body size and ensure proper alignment. If you're unsure how to use a particular piece of equipment, ask a gym staff member or a trainer for guidance.

  • Stay hydrated:

    Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout. Dehydration can lead to decreased performance and potential health risks.

  • Rest between sets:

    Be considerate of other gym-goers. Avoid hogging equipment or occupying multiple pieces of equipment at once. Allow others to work in if necessary and be mindful of personal space.
